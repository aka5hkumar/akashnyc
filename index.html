Hardware

ResoDrill

The purpose of this project was to create a product to help doctors detect resonance frequencies during specific surgeries. A proof of concept was needed for the school to file a provisional patent over the technology. There was another team in Ireland working on the same problem. Most time was spent developing a proprietary solution with various sensors to analyze any data we procured was made and analysis was done after the fact in Matlab. Testing was done in collaboration with the Albert Einstein College of Medicine.

Solar Light

My roommate and I were approached to create a simple and cheap solution to help the UN distribute solar powered lanterns in Africa. Existing solutions were too expensive, too heavy, and over engineered, and we were tasked with creating a working prototype. In the end, while the prototype was successful, developing an effective supply chain proved more difficult than expected. Unfortunately, we weren't able to bring the product to fruition but I learned invaluable lessons about designing for assembly and the importance of international connections.

Software

Uber Movement

Uber has a great program, Uber Movement, that lets individuals and city planners see how congested cities are. However, this data is only available on the website. As a final project, I made an API to make this data accessible to people outside of the private beta and outside of the website.

Rocket Telemetry

I helped a team of Aerospace engineering students build a rocket. I designed, implemented, and tested a functioning telemetry system using various sensors and a raspberry pi. Utilizing GPS, Accelerometers, and cameras was a unique design and testing challenge. I managed to create from scratch a system that could achieve triple redundancy using minimal power. All in just a single week.

Video Slicing

This was a project done for Mango Concepts. I created the backend for and helped design a product that allowed people to type in phrases that spliced together video clips of the presidential candidates. The result was a single video that cut together what the user typed in so long as the candidate had said those words in a speech as some point.
